Empowering public private service providers for improved sanitation to Low income households

Project Background

Kumasi has a dense population with about 37% of inhabitants with limited access to improved home toilets and thus relies heavily on public toilets. The Kumasi Metropolitan Assembly (KMA) wants to achieve a vision where toilets can be found in every compound in Kumasi.continue reading

Enforcement

Approaches toward sanitation service delivery in low-income urban communities (LIUCs) have proven to be more complex than in rural settings. In Kumasi's LIUCs, the complexity of sanitation services delivery is evident, as access to improved sanitation facilities has remained stubbornly low for many years.continue reading

Sani-Marketing

Sanitation marketing is key to the processes and activities for engaging communities to adopt mechanisms to acquire safe household toilets for overall improvement in sanitation at the compound level.
